📊 MGNREGS Performance Analysis Dashboard
📌 Project Overview
This project analyzes the performance of the Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Scheme (MGNREGS) across multiple Indian states over the period 2017–2021. The objective is to identify trends, evaluate efficiency, detect gaps between demand and supply of work, and provide actionable insights for policy improvement.

🎯 Objectives
Analyze employment demand vs supply across states
Measure scheme efficiency using key performance indicators
Identify underperforming regions
Track trends in person-days generated and expenditure
Provide data-driven recommendations
📂 Dataset
Sample structured dataset (synthetic data for analysis)

🔍 Key Insights
Demand for employment consistently exceeds supply across all states
Uttar Pradesh has the highest unmet employment demand
Bihar also shows significant gaps, indicating execution challenges
Tamil Nadu and Karnataka demonstrate relatively better efficiency
Person-days generated and expenditure show a steady increasing trend
Average cost per person-day is approximately ₹1,413, reflecting total expenditure including administrative and material costs
📌 Recommendations
Improve job allocation in high-demand states like Uttar Pradesh and Bihar
Reduce demand-supply gap through better planning and resource allocation
Adopt best practices from high-performing states
Monitor expenditure efficiency for optimal fund utilization
Enhance transparency and tracking using digital systems
🔮 Future Enhancements
Integrate real-time data from government portals
Add district-level drill-down analysis
Include socio-economic indicators (literacy, rural population)
Implement forecasting for future trends
📢 Conclusion
The analysis highlights significant gaps between demand and supply of employment under MGNREGS. While some states demonstrate efficient implementation, others require targeted policy interventions.
