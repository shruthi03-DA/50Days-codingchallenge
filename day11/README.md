📌 Project Overview
This project presents an interactive Insurance Claims & Fraud Analysis Dashboard built using Power BI. The goal is to analyze insurance claim data to identify trends, detect fraud patterns, and provide actionable insights for better decision-making.

#🎯 Objectives
Analyze claim trends over time
Identify high-risk regions and incident types
Detect fraud patterns and contributing factors
Understand relationships between premium, deductible, and claim amount
Provide business insights for risk reduction and cost optimization

#📂 Dataset Description
The dataset contains insurance-related information such as:

Policy details (policy number, state, premium, deductible)
Customer demographics (age, occupation, education)
Incident details (type, severity, location, date)
Claim details (total claim amount, property, injury, vehicle claims)
Fraud status (fraud reported: Yes/No)

#🛠️ Tools & Technologies
Power BI – Data visualization and dashboard creation
Power Query – Data cleaning and transformation
DAX (Data Analysis Expressions) – KPI calculations

#📊 Dashboard Features
🔢 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
Total Claims
Total Claim Amount
Average Claim Amount
Fraud Count
Fraud Rate (%)
Loss Ratio (%)

#📈 Visualizations
Claims Trend Over Time (Line Chart)
Claims Distribution by State (Map)
Total Claim Amount by Incident Type (Bar Chart)
Fraud vs Non-Fraud Analysis (Bar Chart)
Premium vs Claim Amount (Scatter Plot)

#🎛️ Interactive Filters (Slicers)
Time Period (Month/Year)
State
Incident Type
Age Group

#📌 Key Insights
Collision incidents contribute the highest claim amounts
Fraud cases account for approximately 25% of total claims
Certain states show higher claim frequency and fraud rates
Weak correlation observed between premium and claim amount
High-value claims are more likely to be associated with fraud

Short Summary: “We analyzed insurance claims data to identify trends, risk areas, and fraud patterns. The analysis shows that certain regions and policy types contribute disproportionately to claims and losses. Fraud cases are concentrated in specific incident categories, indicating a need for stronger verification processes. Additionally, some segments exhibit high loss ratios, suggesting pricing inefficiencies.”
“Based on these insights, we recommend optimizing premium pricing, enhancing fraud detection, and focusing on low-risk customer segments to improve profitability and reduce losses.”
